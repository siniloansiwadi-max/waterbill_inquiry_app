About Siniloan Water District

History · Mission · Vision · Commitment to community

📜 History of SIWADI

Siniloan Water District (SIWADI) was born out of necessity. Before its creation, the municipal water system – originally built by the Bureau of Public Works and later managed by the municipal government – suffered from poor water quality, low pressure, and unreliable supply. In 1988, Ms. Sonia Costelo of LWUA proposed forming a water district under PD 198. A public hearing on April 29, 1988 at the Leonides de Leon Hall showed strong public support.

On January 18, 1989, the Sangguniang Bayan of Siniloan under Mayor Cenon C. Realeza passed Resolution No. 19 formally creating the Siniloan Water District. Five directors were appointed: Mr. Juan O. Javier, Mr. Jose M. Reyes, Engr. Isidoro V. Realeza, Mrs. Evelia V. Religioso, and Atty. Esther R. Realeza. After Engr. Realeza resigned from the board to become the first General Manager, Engr. Alden R. Mayor filled his seat.

Conditional Certificate of Conformance No. 395 was issued on April 24, 1989. SIWADI took over a dilapidated system: only 227 flat‑rate connections (no meters), no funds, no office, and just one employee – the General Manager. The municipal government provided a P500/month subsidy for one year.

Rehabilitation & Growth

A P2.6 million loan from LWUA (approved Sept. 1990) enabled construction of pumping stations, disinfection, leak repair, and equipment purchase. Full operation began August 23, 1991. Subsequent loans and corporate funds expanded service to fourteen barangays and later to the Municipality of Famy. By 2012, connections exceeded 4,500, and SIWADI was classified as a Category C water district (up from Small to Big in 2010).

Key supporters: Gov. Joey D. Lina (P1M calamity aid, 1996), Congressman Magdaleno Palacol (P900,000 CDF, 1997), LWUA, and loyal consumers.

🎯 Mission

To provide dependable supply of safe, clean and potable water to our clientele in an efficient, responsive and affordable manner.

🌟 Vision

Siniloan Water District will be a dynamic partner dedicated to providing essential services that improve the quality of life, health, and sanitation of our community.
